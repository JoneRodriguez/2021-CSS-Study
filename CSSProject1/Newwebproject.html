Melanie Adele Martinez is an American singer, songwriter, actress, director, photographer, screenwriter, and visual artist. Born in Astoria, Queens, and raised in Baldwin, New York, Martinez rose to prominence in 2012 after appearing on the American television vocal talent show The Voice. Her main albums are CryBaby- which debuted in 2015. Then K-12 which was a movie she debuted in 2019. Last is AfterSchool- an EP that debuted in 2020. Her music is usually about the grim realities of childhood and growing up whilst also putting attention towards issues that girls face in society today. This can range from sexual harassment, dress codes, body image, and stereotypes. She tells all of this by making music about a character she created- CryBaby.
